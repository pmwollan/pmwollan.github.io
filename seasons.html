Definition

The Earth's axis is slightly tilted in relation to its orbit around the Sun. This is why we have seasons. How exactly do seasons work? In most cultures, including all western countries, the year is commonly divided into four seasons: Spring Summer Fall or Autumn Winter

Astronomical Seasons

The astronomical definition uses the dates of equinoxes and solstices to mark the beginning and end of the seasons: Spring begins on the spring equinox; Summer begins on the summer solstice; Fall (autumn) begins on the fall equinox; and Winter begins on the winter solstice. The beginning of each season marks the end of the last. Because the timings of the equinoxes and solstices change each year, the length of astronomical seasons within a year and between years also vary.
